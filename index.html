The Future-Ready School District

This interactive guide outlines the "AI Innovation Challenge." The premise: it's 2035, and a one-time grant has been awarded. Each team must develop a compelling, AI-driven proposal to transform a key area of their work. The catch... the funds are only allocated to a single initiative - the team that can best demonstrate their vision will win the grant! Be creative and thoughtful in your approach ◌ → ◘ pushing the boundaries of what's possible with AI in education.

Engage with AI

Provide hands-on experience with diverse AI tools for text, image, and more.

Foster Collaboration

Encourage communication and teamwork between district departments.

Spark Innovation

Inspire new ideas for applying AI productively in education.

The Challenge & The Teams

The core task is to develop a compelling 7 to 10 minute pitch for an AI-driven proposal. Participants will work in their departmental teams to identify a problem and design an innovative solution.

Activity Phases & Timeline

The challenge unfolds in three distinct phases. The chart below visualizes the time commitment for each part of the activity, helping you manage the flow of the day. Click on each phase to expand and see the detailed steps.

1. Welcome & Overview (15 mins)

  • Review the "Future-Ready School District" premise and goals.
  • Review available AI tools, with emphasize exploration over expertise.
  • Participants will be divided into teams.

2. Departmental Brainstorm (45 mins)

Teams identify a core challenge or opportunity. You may use the prompts below as a starting point for exploration. You should define the problem, your AI vision, and the potential impact.

Operations: How can AI optimize maintenance, security, or transport?

Human Resources: How can AI revolutionize recruitment or professional development?

Special Education: How can AI personalize learning for diverse needs or streamline IEPs?

Finance: How can AI enhance budget allocation or grant management?

Education: How can AI personalize learning paths or automate teacher tasks?

School Leadership: How can AI support school-wide decisions or parent communication?

1. Deep Dive & Solution Design (90 mins)

Teams should use AI tools to build out your proposal. Your challenge is to explore these categories—try out tools, dig around—and see what’s possible. But don’t stop there! Can you discover tools or uses of AI that go beyond these four areas? Think creatively and see where it takes you.

  • Refine problem statement and solution.
  • Draft a short summary or elevator pitch.
  • Write a hypothetical press release.
  • Generate a short script for a promotional video.
  • Create a hypothetical user interface (UI) for the tool.
  • Generate a "before and after" visual comparison.
  • Design a promotional graphic for the program.
  • Imagine a futuristic school environment enhanced by the AI.
  • Create a short explainer video from your script.
  • (Simpler alternative) Use presentation software with text-to-speech to animate slides and images.
  • Generate inspiring background music for the presentation.
  • Create a hypothetical jingle for the AI program.

2. Presentation Preparation (30 mins)

Teams assemble all their AI-generated assets into a creative and cohesive 7 to 10-minute pitch to sell their innovation to the "District Board."

1. Departmental Pitches (45-60 mins)

Each team presents their 5-7 minute pitch. Constructive feedback and questions from the audience (the "District Board") are encouraged after the presentation.

2. Group Reflection & Discussion (15 mins)

After all presentations, lead a group discussion using the following questions:

  • What was the most surprising thing you learned or created with AI today?
  • How did using these tools challenge your preconceptions about AI?
  • Where do you see the most immediate and impactful applications of AI in your role?
  • What ethical considerations must we be mindful of?
  • What's one actionable step you might take to explore AI further in the in your department, as a leadership team, and/or in the District?
